About

Hyojin Kim is a computer scientist in the Center for Applied Scientific Computing at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of California, Davis, in 2012. He received a M.S. in Computer Science from the University of New Hampshire in 2008, and a B.S. in Systems Management Engineering from Sungkyunkwan University in 2001. He was a software engieer with 6+years of experience in GIS/mapping, navigation software development in Korea.

His research interests are in the broad areas of computer vision, image understanding and machine learning including image segmentation, image classification, object recognition, deep neural network, multi-view stereo and 3D reconstruction, multi-modal imagery registration, wide area aerial surveillance, and computerized tomography. He have leveraged GPU techniques to problems in these areas.
